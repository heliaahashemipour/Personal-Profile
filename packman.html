Project Summery:

The Pac-Man projects were developed as an integral part of UC Berkeley's introductory artificial intelligence course, CS 188. These projects encompass a wide array of AI techniques expertly applied to the dynamic realm of Pac-Man gameplay.

Exploration & Search

One facet of my work involved the strategic implementation of exploration and search algorithms. This entailed proficiently executing depth-first, breadth-first, uniform cost, and A* search algorithms. These techniques were employed to address complex navigation challenges and effectively tackle intricate traveling salesman problems within the Pac-Man virtual environment.

Multiagent Strategies

My expertise extended to devising strategies for multiagent scenarios. Within the framework of Classic Pac-Man, I adeptly handled both adversarial and stochastic search situations. This encompassed the seamless implementation of multiagent minimax and expectimax algorithms, complemented by the creation of sophisticated evaluation functions.

Reinforcement Mastery

The pinnacle of my involvement revolved around the mastery of reinforcement learning. I successfully implemented both model-based and model-free reinforcement learning algorithms, skillfully applied to diverse scenarios. These included the AIMA textbook's Gridworld, Pac-Man simulations, and the emulation of a crawling robot's behavior.
